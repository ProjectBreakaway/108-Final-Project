This is a fascinating question. From an existentialist perspective, one might argue that creating AI systems actually represents an attempt to avoid the anxiety of human freedom. By delegating decisions to algorithms, we're essentially creating what Sartre would call "bad faith" - pretending our choices are determined rather than freely made.

However, I'd suggest that existentialism could actually provide a framework for more ethical AI development by emphasizing:

  1. Transparency about the human responsibility behind all AI systems
  2. Designing systems that enhance rather than limit human freedom
  3. Recognizing that no AI can truly "decide" in the existential sense - all outcomes ultimately trace back to human choices
